FERRET BEULAR

The ferret (Mustela putorius furo) is the
domesticated form of the European polecat, a mammal
belonging to the same genus as the
weasel, Mustela of the family Mustelidae.[1] They
typically have brown, black, white, or mixed fur.
They have an average length of 51 cm (20 in) including a 13 cm (5.1 in) tail,
weigh about 1.5–4 pounds (0.7–2 kg), and have a natural lifespan of 7 to 10 years.
Ferrets are sexually dimorphic predators with males being
substantially larger than females.
